In 2015, phishing emails purported to be from “support@gebiz.gov.sg”, are being sent to users. The contents of the email are as follows:

Incident Handling

Dear Partner,

The Infocomm Development Authority of Singapour (IDA) shared that more than 530,000 users have completed their one-time account update since the enhenced SingnetPass was loaunnched last week.

If you fail to update your Singnet Pass account, you will not be able to conduct any business.

Click on the link below to update your SingnetPass account now

http://update.sunsetgates.com/update/server/admindesk/index.htm

What will happen if we click on the phishing url? Proceed to the next page.

Incident Handling

User credentials could be stolen if users enter in their username and password into the phishing page.

What should you do if you have received a phishing email? Proceed to the next page.

Incident Handling

  1. Change your password immediately if you have entered in your user credentials to the phishing page.
  2. If the same password is used across many services (e.g. Gmail, Hotmail, Yahoo), those services passwords must be changed as well.
  3. Do not click on the links in suspicious emails.

Who do you report to when you receive a phishing message? Proceed to the next page.

Incident Handling

Do not respond to them or click on any hyperlink in the email.

Anyone who has received a suspicious email, letter, SMS or phone call should immediately contact your ISP (Internet Service Provider) or your organisation’s IT administration department.

The organisation or person reporting the incident will need to send an email to SingCERT providing the following information:
  1. Contact information including name, email addresses, telephone and fax numbers.
  2. Description of the incident including supporting logs and details such as source and targeted IP addresses, time of occurrence, parties involved and other relevant information
